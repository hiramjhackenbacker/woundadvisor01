Dressing Selection Quick Guide

Match wound characteristics to the most appropriate dressing category.

Dry / Low Exudate

  • Simple island dressing
  • Non-adherent contact layer
  • Hydrocolloid
  • Film/membrane

Medium Exudate

  • Hydrofiber (e.g. Aquacel)
  • Alginate
  • Foam dressing

High Exudate

  • Foam dressing
  • Alginate
  • Hydrofiber

Necrotic / Sloughy

  • Hydrogel
  • Hydrofiber
  • Alginate

Infected Wound

  • Antimicrobial (silver/honey/iodine)
  • Seek wound care specialist

Malodorous

  • Odour absorbent (CarboFLEX)
  • Antimicrobial dressing
Drug Calculations

Drug Calculations

Core formulas with worked examples and interactive calculators. Always estimate before calculating, then verify your answer.

Formula 1 — Tablets / Oral & Injectable Doses (SUNRISE / SR over SS)
Volume Required = Strength Required (SR) Stock Strength (SS) × Volume of Stock

💡 Memory tip: SUNRISE (SR) over SUNSET (SS) — the required strength is always on top.

Formula 2 — IV Drip Rate (Drops Per Minute)
Drip Rate (DPM) = Volume (mL) × Drops/mL Time (h) × 60

DPM = drops per minute. Always convert litres to mL (×1000) before calculating.

Formula 3 — IV Infusion: Volume, Rate & Time
Volume (mL) = Rate (mL/h) × Time (h)   |   Rate = Volume (mL) Time (h)   |   Time = Volume (mL) Rate (mL/h)

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Injection from Stock Solution: Pethidine 70mg from 10g/200mL ▼
  1. Identify: Strength Required (SR) = 70 mg. Stock Strength (SS) = 10 g in 200 mL.
  2. Convert units: 10 g → 10 × 1000 = 10,000 mg (units must match).
  3. Estimate: 70 mg is a small fraction of 10,000 mg, so expect much less than 200 mL.
  4. Apply formula: Volume = (70 ÷ 10,000) × 200 = 14,000 ÷ 10,000 = 1.4 mL
  5. Check: 1.4 mL is much less than 200 mL — matches our estimate. ✔
✅ Answer: 1.4 mL
Example 2 — IV Drip Rate: 1.5 L over 10 hours, giving set 20 drops/mL ▼
  1. Identify: Volume = 1.5 L, Drops/mL = 20, Time = 10 hours.
  2. Convert: 1.5 L × 1000 = 1500 mL
  3. Estimate: 150 mL/h × (20/60) ≈ 50 drops/min expected.
  4. Apply formula: DPM = (1500 × 20) ÷ (10 × 60) = 30,000 ÷ 600 = 50 dpm
  5. Check: Matches estimate. ✔
✅ Answer: 50 drops per minute
Example 3 — Oral Tablets: 750 mg ciprofloxacin, 500 mg tablets available ▼
  1. Identify: SR = 750 mg, SS = 500 mg per tablet. Volume of stock = 1 (tablets are not in solution).
  2. Estimate: 500 mg = 1 tablet, 750 mg = more than 1 but less than 2 tablets.
  3. Apply formula: Tablets = (750 ÷ 500) × 1 = 1.5 tablets
  4. Check: Matches estimate — 1 whole tablet + half tablet. ✔
✅ Answer: 1.5 tablets
Example 4 — IV Infusion Volume: 100 mL/h for 1.5 hours ▼
  1. Identify: Rate = 100 mL/h, Time = 1.5 h. Finding total Volume.
  2. Estimate: 100 mL in 1 h + 50 mL in 0.5 h = ~150 mL.
  3. Apply formula: Volume = 100 × 1.5 = 150 mL
  4. Check: Matches estimate. ✔
✅ Answer: 150 mL
Example 5 — Infusion Duration: 2 L dextrose saline at 160 mL/h ▼
  1. Identify: Volume = 2 L = 2000 mL, Rate = 160 mL/h. Finding Time.
  2. Estimate: 2000 ÷ 160 ≈ a bit more than 12 hours.
  3. Apply formula: Time = 2000 ÷ 160 = 12.5 hours
  4. Check: 12.5 h × 160 mL/h = 2000 mL ✔
✅ Answer: 12.5 hours (12 h 30 min)

Sepsis Recognition & Management

Sepsis is a life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection. Early recognition saves lives.

⚠️

Think Sepsis — Act Fast

Sepsis can deteriorate rapidly. If suspected, escalate immediately and initiate the sepsis bundle within the first hour. Every hour of delay increases mortality.

Signs & Symptoms

Adult Symptoms — SEPSIS Mnemonic
  • SSlurred speech or confusion
  • EExtreme shivering or muscle pain
  • PPassing no urine in a day
  • SSevere breathlessness
  • I"It feels like you're going to die" — impending doom
  • SSkin mottled, discoloured or pale
Child Symptoms
  • 🔴Has a fit or convulsion
  • 🔵Looks mottled, bluish or pale
  • 🔴Has a rash that does not fade when pressed (non-blanching)
  • 🟡Very lethargic or difficult to wake
  • ❄️Feels abnormally cold to touch

Additional Indicators

Fever >38°C or <36°C Heart rate >90 bpm Resp rate >20 breaths/min WBC >12 or <4 × 10⁹/L Altered mental status Sweaty / diaphoretic skin Hypotension (MAP <65 mmHg) Lactate >2 mmol/L

The 1-Hour Sepsis Bundle

Initiate within 1 hour of recognition (Surviving Sepsis Campaign) ESCALATE IMMEDIATELY
1
Measure Lactate
Remeasure if >2 mmol/L. Lactate >4 = septic shock marker.
2
Blood Cultures
Obtain before starting antibiotics (≥2 sets)
3
Broad-Spectrum Antibiotics
Administer IV broad-spectrum antibiotics immediately
4
IV Fluid Resuscitation
30 mL/kg crystalloid if hypotensive or lactate ≥4
5
Vasopressors
Noradrenaline if MAP <65 mmHg despite fluids
6
Monitor & Reassess
Continuous obs, urine output, repeat lactate, escalation

qSOFA Quick Screen

Score 1 point for each. Score ≥2 = high risk of poor outcome — escalate immediately.

Parameter Threshold for 1 Point Clinical Significance
Altered mentation GCS < 15 Confusion, agitation, reduced responsiveness
Respiratory rate ≥ 22 breaths/min Tachypnoea indicating respiratory compromise
Systolic BP ≤ 100 mmHg Hypotension indicating circulatory compromise

Key Nursing Actions

Recognise Early

  • Use qSOFA / NEWS2 score
  • Think sepsis in any deteriorating patient
  • Check for infection source

Escalate Immediately

  • Inform senior nurse / MET / ICU
  • Document time of recognition
  • Initiate sepsis pathway

Monitoring

  • Continuous vital signs
  • Strict urine output measurement
  • Repeat lactate if elevated

Fluid Management

  • Large bore IV access ×2
  • 30 mL/kg crystalloid bolus
  • Reassess after each bolus

Anaphylaxis Management

Anaphylaxis is a severe, life-threatening systemic allergic reaction affecting multiple organ systems. Immediate treatment is essential.

⚡ Epi First — Epi Fast!

Epinephrine (adrenaline) is the first-line treatment. Use it immediately at the first sign of anaphylaxis. Any delay can worsen outcomes.

Adrenaline 0.5 mg IM (Adult) — Anterolateral Thigh — No Bolus IV

Common Triggers / Allergens

🥜 Food

  • Peanuts
  • Tree nuts (almonds, cashews, walnuts)
  • Shellfish & fish
  • Cow's milk / eggs
  • Soy / wheat / sesame

💊 Medication

  • Penicillin
  • Aspirin
  • Ibuprofen & NSAIDs
  • Contrast media

🐝 Venom

  • Yellow jackets
  • Wasps & hornets
  • Honeybees
  • Fire ants & spiders

🧤 Latex

  • Rubber gloves
  • Balloons
  • Elastic bands
  • Cross-reactive: banana, kiwi, avocado

Symptoms by Body System

🫁 Respiratory / Throat

Tightness, hoarseness, difficulty swallowing, stridor, wheeze, shortness of breath, cough

💓 Cardiovascular

Weak/rapid pulse, dizziness, passing out, hypotension, cardiac arrest

🩺 Skin

Itching, hives (urticaria), redness, swelling, flushing, angioedema

👄 Mouth

Itching, swelling of lips and/or tongue, tingling

🫃 Gastrointestinal

Vomiting, diarrhoea, cramping, nausea, abdominal pain

🧠 Neurological

Feeling of impending doom, headache, confusion, loss of consciousness

Emergency Management Steps

Immediate Response Protocol
  1. 1
    Remove trigger / call for help

    Stop any infusion, remove the allergen if possible. Call MET / emergency team immediately.

  2. 2
    Administer Adrenaline (Epinephrine) IM

    Adult: 0.5 mg (0.5 mL of 1:1000) IM into anterolateral thigh. Child: 0.01 mg/kg (max 0.5 mg). Repeat every 5–15 min if no improvement. No IV bolus.

  3. 3
    Position patient

    Lie flat with legs elevated (if hypotensive). Sit upright if respiratory distress predominates. Do not allow sudden standing — risk of cardiac arrest.

  4. 4
    High-flow oxygen + airway management

    Administer 100% O₂ via non-rebreather mask. Prepare for intubation if airway compromise is imminent.

  5. 5
    IV access + fluid resuscitation

    Large bore IV. IV fluid bolus (crystalloid 20 mL/kg) for hypotension. Monitor continuously.

  6. 6
    Monitor & reassess

    Continuous vital signs, SpO₂, ECG. 12-lead ECG. Observe for biphasic reaction (up to 72 h post-reaction).

  7. 7
    Secondary medications (after adrenaline)

    Antihistamine (e.g. promethazine). Corticosteroid (e.g. hydrocortisone IV) — does not replace adrenaline but may reduce biphasic reactions. Salbutamol nebuliser for bronchospasm.

⚠️ Refractory Anaphylaxis

If no improvement after 2 IM adrenaline doses — escalate to ICU. Refractory anaphylaxis requires specialised management.

Second-Line Treatments
  • IV titrated adrenaline (no bolus — monitoring required)
  • IV fluid therapy (adequate, with monitoring)
  • Second vasopressor (metaraminol, dopamine)
  • Glucagon if on beta-blockers
  • Methylene blue (case-by-case)
  • Extracorporeal life support (last resort)
Special Considerations
  • Airway: support, ventilation, positioning
  • Patients on beta-blockers may not respond to adrenaline
  • Two autoinjectors needed — symptoms can recur
  • All patients should be observed ≥4–8 hours post-reaction
  • Refer to allergy specialist for follow-up

Patient Education Discharge Points

Always Carry Epi

  • Two adrenaline autoinjectors at all times
  • Know how to use it
  • Teach family members

Identify Triggers

  • Medical alert bracelet
  • Read food labels carefully
  • Inform all healthcare providers

Action Plan

  • Written anaphylaxis action plan
  • Know when to use epi vs antihistamine
  • Always call 000 after epi use

Follow Up

  • Allergy/immunology referral
  • Consider immunotherapy
  • Review and update action plan yearly
