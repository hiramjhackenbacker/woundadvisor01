📋 What is ABPI?

The Ankle–Brachial Pressure Index (ABPI) compares systolic blood pressure at the ankle to the arm. It is a simple, non-invasive screening test for peripheral artery disease (PAD) and is also used to assess safety before applying compression therapy.

Equipment needed Doppler probe (8–10 MHz), sphygmomanometer, ultrasound gel
Patient position Supine, resting for ≥5 minutes before measurement
Formula Ankle systolic ÷ Brachial systolic = ABPI
Normal range 1.0 – 1.4 (ankle pressure normally exceeds arm)
💡 If wounds or ulcers are present at the cuff site, cover with a protective barrier (e.g. plastic wrap) before applying the cuff. If the patient has significant lower limb pain or cellulitis, proceed with care — the procedure may cause discomfort.
1
Patient Preparation
Position and rest the patient before measuring
▾
Position the patient supine (flat on their back) and allow them to rest for at least 5 minutes before taking any measurements. Remove footwear and socks. Expose both arms and ankles. Apply ultrasound gel to the probe site before each measurement.

Brachial cuff: 2–3 cm above antecubital fossa (elbow crease)

Ankle cuff: 5 cm above medial malleolus (inner ankle bone)

Both readings taken with patient flat. Do not measure sitting or standing.

2
Brachial (Arm) Systolic Pressures
Measure both arms — the higher value is used in the formula
▾
Place the cuff 2–3 cm above the antecubital fossa. Apply gel and locate the brachial artery pulse with the Doppler probe. Inflate the cuff until the Doppler signal disappears, then slowly deflate — the pressure at which you first hear the signal return is the systolic pressure. Record both arms. The highest value will be used in the ABPI calculation.

📊 ABPI Interpretation Reference

> 1.4 Inconclusive Non-compressible vessels (calcification). Refer to vascular lab.
1.0 – 1.4 Normal PAD excluded in most patients. Compression therapy safe if > 0.8.
0.9 Borderline Consider vascular surgery discussion if symptoms/risk factors present.
0.5 – 0.9 Abnormal (PAD) Diagnostic of PAD. Manage CVD risk. Refer if not improving at 6 months.
< 0.5 Critical ischaemia Urgent vascular referral. Do NOT use compression. High amputation risk.
🩹 Compression Therapy Safety Guide ABPI > 0.8 — Full compression hosiery is safe  |  ABPI 0.5–0.8 — High compression NOT recommended (risk of necrosis)  |  ABPI < 0.5 — Compression is contraindicated
